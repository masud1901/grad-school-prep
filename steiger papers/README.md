# Steiger papers

**Juaren Steiger** — Lu lab collaborator (Queen's → Penn State postdoc). First author on the bandit/scheduling papers that your proposal extends: constrained bandits with switching costs (POSS), backlogged bandits, delayed semi-bandit feedback.

Store PDFs and notes for his work here. Reading list: `notes/Lu_and_Beitollahi_Reading_List.md` (Lu-1, Lu-2, Lu-3).

## Ranked reading order (for your proposal)

| Rank | Paper | Venue | Why this order |
|------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **1** | **Constrained Bandit Learning with Switching Costs for Wireless Networks** (POSS) — Steiger, Li, Ji, Lu | INFOCOM 2023 | **Read first.** Your proposal extends POSS directly: you replace the sample mean in the optimistic index with a robust estimator (MoM/Catoni) for heavy-tailed rewards. You need the full algorithm, block structure, and virtual-queue dual. |
| **2** | **Backlogged Bandits: Cost-Effective Learning for Utility Maximization in Queueing Networks** — Steiger, Li, Lu | INFOCOM 2024 | **Read second.** Same team; extends bandits to queueing. Connects to wireless/scheduling and gives you the evolution of the framework. Useful for “where Lu’s bandit work is going.” |
| **3** | **Learning from Delayed Semi-Bandit Feedback under Strong Fairness Guarantees** — Steiger, Li, Lu | INFOCOM 2022 | **Read third.** Same team; delayed feedback + fairness. Builds intuition for the “math tools” and proof style in this line of work; less directly tied to your RQ1 than POSS. |

**Summary:** 1 = must-read (you extend it). 2 = strong context. 3 = same lab, same style — read when you want more depth.
